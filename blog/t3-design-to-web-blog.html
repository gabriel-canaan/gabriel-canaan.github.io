Responsive
As smart phones, with their comparatively small screens, have become a (if not the ) primary access to the Internet; sites are required to perform correctly on this platform.
A full screen layout that necessitates excessive manipulation (often with only a thumb) proves unwieldy when viewed on a format of reduced size.

Mobile first !
With this in mind, the trend, almost exclusively, is towards ‘mobile first’. That is to say that the design and the ‘architecture’ of a site is intended, fundamentally, to be fully operative on a viewing portal of 445px. subsequent expansion to tablet or full HD monitor size can reveal the page in it’s full glory, with additional features and images, but the vital information and interactivity must be available immediately on the most commonly used medium.To neglect this seemingly obvious, yet historically underestimated, aspect of design is at one’s peril.

Frame works ?
In the overarching spirit of community amongst developers, many open source templates, or ‘frameworks’ have been published in the attempt to facilitate the production of high caliber, fully functioning web experiences. Whether this is purely altruistic behavior, or nefarious ulterior motivation to avoid ugly Internet engagements is debatable. Notwithstanding, frameworks provide a 'pret a porter' kit-set webpage for you to add your own content. Ikeaweb.

wireframe
The ‘blueprint’ for a page/site is referred to as a Wire-frame. It designates the lay out of a site in terms of the position and order of the elements that will be presented. It is the first step in the development process and precedes design considerations.
