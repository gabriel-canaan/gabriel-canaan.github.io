Oups .. I read the first few chapters last week and used chapter two as a basis for lot of my last blog. I guess that means I was enjoying it. In the past I have been a bit skeptical of the claims of ‘meditators’ mainly because of their propensity to write throwaway self-help books full of ‘fake news!’. On the other hand the idea of being still an having a calm mind has always appealed to me.

The concept of ‘training’ your mind to seems a bit obvious when you think about it; but the idea that rectifying your ‘mistakes’ in the form of readjustments, when your mind wanders off, will strengthen and build your mental core was a new one to me. Very encouraging as well, as I can honestly give myself an A+ for wandering.

The concentrating on breathing seems to the place to start and working on posture. Unfortunately I won’t be adopting full lotus anytime soon. Identifying distractions, acknowledging them and letting them move off, combined with mindfully ‘creating space between stimulus and reaction’ would be the next step.

I read once that a technique to develop clarity was to imagine a pure white square and to hold it in the minds eye, trying to keep it there unchanging. Ha ! You can probably guess how well thats working out.
