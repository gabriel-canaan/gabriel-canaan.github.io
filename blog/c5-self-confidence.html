Danny and Jonnie. My new buddies. Maybe they just don’t know it yet. Might not even be buddies themselves. They sound the same to me. Both statements refer to a similar, if not the same, state. An oxymoronic sounding: relaxed alertness, calm activity, or an unexpectant anticipation. Self awareness practice.

Body scan is something I cheat can at, or at least I'm familiar with . I didn’t know it was a thing. I do it a bit un/sub/consciously when I want to chill out on a sun lounger or lie down in bed. I’ve a couple of injuries ( nothing heroic I’m afraid ) that I need to take into account. I find that identifying the physical sensations (?) makes it a a bit easier to compartmentalise them and to nullify their influence. Of course it’s a important thing to be aware of: the ‘cause and effect’ of an environment on a physical body, and no better way than to dissociate the mind(fullness) from it than by a methodical recognition of it’s parts. Bloody relaxing as well … in a Zen way.

Emotions .. not me.

I wonder if a subtle change in our language could help us here a little bit. In French, as in loads of other languages I guess, we ‘are’ not an age for example but rather we ‘have ‘ age. I imagine thinking therefore, that I ‘have’ : anger, sadness or inappropriately wild joy after finding the missing semi-colon; not that I ‘am’ any of these emotions. Bearing this in mind I can train myself to ‘not have’ them too. I remain the same. Whoo hoo ! . A quantum mechanic might disagree of course but then you can’t please all the people all the time.
